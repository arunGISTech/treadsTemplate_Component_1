Cooperation within Conflict:

Transboundary Governance in Godavari

TREADS
May 5, 2023

Context

The states of Andhra Pradesh and Telangana share a common history of river development until their bifurcation in 2014. Since then, the states are engaged in a process of negotiating their tenuous transboundary relationships, primarily over the rivers of Krishna and Godavari. The Krishna and Godavari River Management Boards (KRMB, GRMB respectively) were created to facilitate this coordination between the states. The two states have been coordinating the management of the shared rivers through these river boards, albeit with many outstanding contentious issues.

This suffers from a fundamental challenge of non-existence of specified allocations to the states (from those of erstwhile united Andhra Pradeshâ€™s allocations). This puts an unusual burden on the boards. On top of this, the Ministry of Jal Shakti had issued an unprecedented direction in July 2021 with respect to the jurisdictions of the two boards creating a particularly difficult challenge for the boards.

The structure of the boards and the resources at their disposal are no way commensurate with the complex challenges of the above ambiguities. Yet, the experiences of the Boards in addressing the coordination challenges so far are instructive and offer a very interesting experiential wisdom offering key insights for conceiving the much-desired ecosystem for interstate river water cooperation and coordination. This case study is aimed at contributing to this goal.
